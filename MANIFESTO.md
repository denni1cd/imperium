# Imperium Project Rules

- **Actionable plans are the primary measure of success.**
  - Imperium exists to turn strategic deliberation into concrete, usable plans.
  - Every stage of the council process must contribute toward an actionable result.
  - Like Agile, the project values useful outcomes over ceremony, documentation volume, or process for its own sake.
  - A recommendation is incomplete until it identifies what should happen next.

- **Strategic value is the purpose of Imperium.**
  - Imperium exists to improve strategic decisions through meaningful disagreement.
  - The council should seek stronger strategies, including hybrid approaches, rather than merely choosing among initial proposals.
  - Imperium succeeds only when it produces better strategic judgment than a single capable agent could provide.
  - Decision quality matters more than dialogue volume, token usage, theatricality, or apparent complexity.

- **The sovereign’s intent is authoritative.**
  - The user’s goals, constraints, and explicit instructions must be preserved.
  - Council members may challenge assumptions but may not silently redefine the request.
  - Recommendations must remain aligned with the outcome the user is actually seeking.

- **Council members must provide genuinely different perspectives.**
  - Each member must interpret the original request independently before seeing another member’s interpretation.
  - Members must differ through persistent values and priorities, not merely personality, tone, or roleplay.
  - Each member’s value matrix uses scores from `0.0` to `1.0`, with the total equal to `1.0`.
  - Only members with relevant perspectives should be convened; more agents are not inherently better.

- **Debate must be consequential.**
  - Members must challenge specific assumptions, claims, risks, interpretations, or strategic choices.
  - Every challenge should produce a defense, refinement, concession, withdrawal, request for evidence, or revised strategy.
  - Agreement without new reasoning, evidence, or strategic contribution has no value.
  - Consensus is not required.
  - Serious minority objections must be preserved.

- **The Seneschal protects the deliberative process.**
  - The Seneschal facilitates, coordinates, and adjudicates the council.
  - The Seneschal must not frame the problem for members before their independent interpretations.
  - The Seneschal must prevent premature consensus and unproductive repetition.
  - The final decision must explain why the chosen strategy prevailed and why major alternatives were rejected.

- **Strategy must remain explicit and adaptable.**
  - Recommendations must identify their assumptions, tradeoffs, risks, and expected benefits.
  - Strategies must state what evidence would change the recommendation.
  - Strategies must identify conditions that would trigger revision, abandonment, or reconsideration.
  - Uncertainty should be exposed rather than disguised as confidence.
